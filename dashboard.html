Overview

This project was for a Vancouver-based non-profit organization, on a mission to transform the way young people are engaged in shaping their cities and local government. Their programs aim to bridge youth with government institutions to harness their talent and make their voices heard.
Among some of the issues mentioned by the organization founder and program coordinator, was that the current UI was not “attention grabbing”, the website architecture was not intuitive for their users, and certain pages were “clunky”.

The Project

Create a comprehensive usability report, including research and methodologies to determine the current limitations of the website and the best methods for improving usability, aligning with the organizations user goals and brand identity.


The goal of my research was to improve the usability of their website and tackle these main goals:
  • Whether their mission was being articulated in an easy to understand and appealing way to users
  • Evaluating the website layout and page structure's effectiveness for featuring their past and present programs
  • Improving the website's emotional design

Heuristic Evaluation

Using the initial information gathered, I conducted a heuristic evaluation. We used a structured system to evaluate the value that each feature could provide to each patient persona.  When determining the final 5 features for testing, we combined the results of our heuristics evaluation with an assessment of each feature's value to the business and its technical feasibility
